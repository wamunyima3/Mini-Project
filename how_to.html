GRADING POINT AVERAGE (GPA)

HOW TO CALCULATE THE GPA

Grade Point Average, or GPA, is a numerical representation of a student's academic performance in college or university. It is a critical metric that is often used to evaluate a student's academic progress, and it is used by many institutions to determine eligibility for scholarships, grants, and other academic opportunities. In this essay, we will discuss the process of calculating GPA.

The first step in calculating GPA is to assign a numerical value to each letter grade. The most common scale assigns the following values: A = 4.0, A- = 3.7, B+ = 3.3, B = 3.0, B- = 2.7, C+ = 2.3, C = 2.0, C- = 1.7, D+ = 1.3, D = 1.0, F = 0.0. Once the letter grade is assigned, it is converted to its numerical value for calculation purposes.

The second step is to determine the credit value of each course. Credit hours are determined based on the amount of time spent in class per week. For example, a 3-credit course typically meets for three hours per week.

The third step involves multiplying the numerical grade value by the credit value for each course. For example, if a student received a B (3.0) in a 3-credit course, the calculation would be: 3.0 (numerical grade value) x 3 (credit value) = 9.0 (grade points earned). This step is repeated for every course taken in the semester or academic year.

The fourth step is to add up all the grade points earned. The total grade points earned for each course during the semester or academic year are added together.

The final step is to divide the total grade points earned by the total credit hours attempted. For example, if a student earned 30 grade points in a semester with 12 credit hours attempted, the calculation would be: 30 (total grade points earned) / 12 (total credit hours attempted) = 2.5 (GPA for the semester).
